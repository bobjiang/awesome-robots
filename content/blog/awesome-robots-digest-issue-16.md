## Tool/Resource of the Week üõ†Ô∏è

### üéØ Featured Resource: **Intel RealSense Depth Cameras**

**Multiple CES 2026 robots (EngineAI T800, SwitchBot Onero H1) leverage Intel RealSense D415 and D435i cameras for depth perception and 3D environmental mapping, making this the de facto standard for robotic vision systems.**

**Key Features:**
- **D415**: Optimal for precise measurements and flat surface detection with narrow field of view
- **D435i**: Wide 87¬∞ FOV with built-in IMU for SLAM applications and dynamic movement
- **Depth Technology**: Active IR stereo with up to 10m range and sub-millimeter accuracy

**Why It's Useful:**
RealSense cameras provide turnkey depth perception without requiring custom vision pipelines. The standardization across multiple robot manufacturers means abundant community resources, pre-trained models, and proven reliability in production environments. On-device processing eliminates cloud dependencies while maintaining real-time performance.

**Getting Started:**
- **Website:** [https://www.intelrealsense.com/](https://www.intelrealsense.com/)
- **Documentation:** [Intel RealSense SDK 2.0](https://github.com/IntelRealSense/librealsense)
- **Community:** [RealSense GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/IntelRealSense/librealsense/discussions)

**Use Cases:**
- Household object recognition and manipulation (SwitchBot Onero H1)
- Industrial obstacle avoidance and navigation (EngineAI T800)
- 3D mapping for SLAM and autonomous navigation
- Hand-eye coordination for robotic arms
- People detection and tracking for safety systems
